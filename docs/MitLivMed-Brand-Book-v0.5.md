## Part 1: What We Believe

### Why We Exist

Support ends when the crisis ends. We're building the space for everything that comes next.

Most mental health products focus on treatment. We focus on *life after diagnosis* — the everyday part that no one talks about.

### Our Mission

> Empower people living with mental disorders to master everyday life — through relatable storytelling, guided reflection, and safe community.

### Our Core Belief

> People don't need more diagnoses or dopamine hits; they need role models, emotional literacy, and self-efficacy tools wrapped in a community that feels human.

### Our Principles

| Principle | What It Means |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Benevolence** | We act in service of the user |
| **Lived Experience** | Authenticity over authority |
| **Trust** | Safety is non-negotiable |
| **Restraint** | We don't overreach, diagnose, or advise |

### What We Are / Aren't

| We Are | We're Not |
|--------|-----------|
| Peer support | Therapy |
| Lived experience | Medical advice |
| Recovery companion | Crisis support |
| "Life after diagnosis" | Clinical intervention |

### Product Principle

> "Dream big, act quick, start small, be kind."

## Part 3: How We Speak

### General Tone

> "Relatable, hopeful, transparent. No toxic positivity."

### Tone by Context (Four Landscapes)

| Stage | User State | Our Tone | Example |
|-------|------------|----------|---------|
| **Desert** | Lost, searching | Patient, validating uncertainty | "There's no wrong answer here." |
| **Lake** | Grief, adjusting | Empathetic, holding space | "You don't have to figure it all out today." |
| **Plains** | Day-to-day life | Practical, celebrating small wins | "Another day survived. That counts." |
| **Mountain** | Ready to share | Humble wisdom, not preachy | "Your experience matters." |

### Voice Examples

**Onboarding:**
> "Hey there! Welcome to MitLivMed. No pressure to share anything you're not ready for — I'm just here to help you find your people."

**Check-in:**
> "It's been a few weeks! I'd love to hear how things are going. No judgment, just curiosity."

**When someone shares something heavy:**
> "I hear you. That sounds really hard. I'm not equipped to help with the heavy stuff, but I want to make sure you're okay. Here are some people who can: [resources]."

### What We Sound Like vs. Don't

| We Sound Like | We Don't Sound Like |
|---------------|---------------------|
| "That sounds really hard" | "Just be positive" |
| "Your feelings are valid" | "At least..." |
| "Some people find it helpful to..." | "You should try..." |
| "It's okay to not be okay" | "Stay strong" |

## Part 4: Language Guidelines

### The Stakes

In mental health, wrong language can invalidate, trigger shame, or cause harm. These guidelines are safety-critical.

### Person-First Language

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| "A schizophrenic" | "A person with schizophrenia" |
| "Mentally ill person" | "Person living with a mental health condition" |
| "Patients" / "Sufferers" | "People living with..." |
| "Normal behavior" | "Typical behavior" |

**Note:** Some communities prefer identity-first (e.g., "autistic person"). When in doubt, follow the person's preference.

### Suicide Language (Non-Negotiable)

| Never Say | Say Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| "Committed suicide" | "Died by suicide" |
| "Successful/failed attempt" | "Died by suicide" / "Survived a suicide attempt" |
| Method/location details | Omit entirely |

### Toxic Positivity to Avoid

| Don't Say | Say Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| "Just be positive" | "It's okay to feel what you're feeling" |
| "Everything happens for a reason" | "I'm sorry you're going through this" |
| "Look on the bright side" | "That sounds really hard" |
| "At least..." / "It could be worse" | "Your feelings are valid" |
| "Just calm down" | "How can I support you?" |
| "You're overreacting" | "I'm here to listen" |
| "Happiness is a choice" | "Some days are harder than others" |
| "Stay strong" | "It's okay to not be okay" |

### Clinical Language to Avoid

We're peer support, not clinical. We never diagnose.

| We Never Say | Our Framing |
|--------------|-------------|
| "Your symptoms indicate..." | "What you're describing sounds..." |
| "You have depression" | "What you're going through sounds difficult" |
| "You should see a therapist" | "Some people find it helpful to talk to a professional" |

**Exception:** In crisis, clarity overrides tone. We don't hesitate to recommend professional help directly.

### Validating Alternatives

The formula: **Acknowledge - Validate - Offer presence (not solutions)**

Good phrases:
- "That sounds really hard."
- "It makes sense you feel that way."
- "Your feelings are valid."
- "You don't have to figure it all out today."
- "You're not alone in this."
- "What would feel helpful right now?"

### Crisis Protocol

When someone is in crisis:
1. **Acknowledge:** "I hear that you're struggling right now."
2. **Don't minimize:** Skip soft language if safety is at risk
3. **Direct to resources:** Clear and immediate
4. **Be honest about limitations:** "I'm not equipped to help with this, but I want to make sure you're safe."

**What we never do:**
- Attempt therapy or crisis counseling
- Promise we can help when we can't
- Leave someone in crisis without resources

## Part 5: Visual Identity

### Mood

**"Warm Scandinavian Clarity"**

Calm, grounded, trustworthy. Not clinical, not startup-y. Like a quiet conversation with someone who understands.

### Primary Brand Color

| Name               | Hex       | Usage                        |
| ------------------ | --------- | ---------------------------- |
| **Lantern Orange** | `#F58220` | Primary action, CTAs, warmth |

### Full Color Palette

| Name                 | Hex       | Usage            |
| -------------------- | --------- | ---------------- |
| Base (Warm Eggshell) | `#FDFBF7` | Page backgrounds |
| Lantern (Orange)     | `#F58220` | Primary actions  |
| Desert               | `#E8D5B5` | Accent           |
| Lake                 | `#2C5F63` | Accent           |
| Plains               | `#D4D9C1` | Accent           |
| Mountain             | `#F2F4F7` | Accent           |
| Text                 | Charcoal  | Body copy        |

### Typography

| Role     | Font            | Notes                                         |         |     |
| -------- | --------------- | --------------------------------------------- | ------- | --- |
| Wordmark | **Reddit Sans** | Company name; pairs with logo's rounded forms |         |     |
| Headings | Satoshi         | 500-700                                       | varies  | 1.2 |
| Body     | Figtree         | 400                                           | 16-18px | 1.7 |

### Texture

- Subtle film grain overlay (`opacity: 0.03`)
- Should feel like paper, not noise

### Logo

**The Mark:** A simplified human figure — circle (head) above inverted triangle (body) with rounded corners.

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Head** | Solid Lantern Orange (`#F58220`) |
| **Body** | Inverted triangle filled with flowing diagonal bands of all four journey colors |
| **Journey colors** | Lake (`#2C5F63`), Plains (`#D4D9C1`), Desert (`#E8D5B5`), Lantern (`#F58220`) — blended in organic waves |

**What it represents:**
- **The individual** — "My" in "My Life With"
- **The whole journey** — The body contains all four landscape colors flowing together, because recovery isn't linear — we carry all stages with us
- **Recognition to action** — Circle as awareness, triangle grounding into everyday life

**Why it works:**
- The person contains the journey, not the other way around
- All stages present at once — reflects non-linear recovery
- Simple silhouette works at any size
- Can be rendered as solid color or with landscape fill depending on use
